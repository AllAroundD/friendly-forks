Welcome to Friendly-Forks

Are you a student or in Toronto temporarily, and would like to connect with a local?

Try Friendly-Forks. We pair students and travellers with local hosts who open their homes and their hearts to share a fun filled meal over lunch, dinner or breakfast with with guests and travellers from all over the world.

If you like to meet people and share stories and experiences, Friendly-Forks is for you.

Is Friendly-Forks a restaurant?

No. There is no monetary exchange involved. Although you are welcome to take a gift for your host, it is not mandatory. Just be ready to talk, share and enjoy the company.

Then why not just go to a restaurant?

Everything you may wat to learn about Toronto, Canada or Canadians, you can from wikipedia. However, what you can't get from wikipedia or the internet are the stories and experiences that your host and guests will bring to the table.

Would you like to meet Roya?

One of our hosts, Roya Seddiqi came to Toronto from Afghanistan in 2007. Her first job was at a Tamil restaurant. Where she was introduced to the spices of South Asia. Inspiring her to create Kuttu-Kabbab-Roti. Which mixes the traditional Afghan kabab with Tamil Kuttu-Roti. She hopes to start her own restaurant after COVID19 is over.

Would you like to meet Ron & Igor?

Ron used to be an international Journalists who has travelled and worked all over the world. However, an injury in 2011 made him settle in Toronto. It is here that he met his partner Igor from Brazil.

Would you like to see Toronto through a local's eye?

Each spring thousands of people folk to High Park to see the Cherry Blossom bloom. Unknown to most but equally enthralling are the Cherry Blossom orchads at York University.
